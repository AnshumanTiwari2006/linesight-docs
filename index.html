quickstart.py 
from linesight import LinearRegression

model = LinearRegression(
    learning_rate=0.01,
    epochs=1000,
    store_history=True
)
model.fit(X, y)

# See your equation
model.show_equation()
→  ŷ = 2.01x + 0.98

# Understand the fit
model.explain_fit(X, y)
→  R² = 0.998 — explains 99.8% of variance

# Watch it learn
model.animate_training(X, y)

Architecture

Three Transparent Layers

Every model in LineSight is decomposed into three clearly separated layers — computation, interpretation, and visualization — so you always know exactly what is happening and why.

engine/

The numerical core. Clean, readable implementations of gradient descent and coordinate descent that expose named weights and bias parameters instead of opaque theta vectors.

explain/

Natural-language interpretation layer. Automatically translates coefficients, regularization effects, and decision boundaries into human-readable explanations with contextual annotations.

visualization/

Publication-quality matplotlib visualizations and animations. Includes loss surfaces, residual diagnostics, training convergence animations, prediction intervals, and 30+ additional methods.
